Division of labor

Building a game requires a vast range of knowledge, from C++ programming to quaternion exponentiation to color theory to writing snappy dialog. Even starting from a modern game engine, the expertise required is still daunting. Very few people have the expertise to do everything involved in developing a game. Even if they did, most games require many person-years of effort. Very large games can require thousands of person-years. Human lifespan is wildly insufficient to make Grand Theft Auto V on oneâ€™s own.

So realistically, most games, even small indie games, are built by diverse groups of specialists. Not all are programmers, and often only one is a programmer.
